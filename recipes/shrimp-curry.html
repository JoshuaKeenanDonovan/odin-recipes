Coconut Shrimp Curry

A tangy shrimp dish with a creamy sauce with some kick that is best served over rice

Indgredients

For Shrimp Marinade

  • 1 pound extra-large shrimp, peeled and devined
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black, freshly ground
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 tablespoon lemon juice

For the sauce

  • 1 tablespoon cocnut oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, fresly ground
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 2 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 14.5 ounce diced tomatoes
  • 13.5 ounce cocnut milk
  • 2 tablespoon cilantro or parsley, for garnish
  • cooked rice for serving

Shrimp Marinade

  • Shrimp --Extra large, raw, deshellled and deveined
  • Seasoning --Salt and pepper
  • Spices --Cayenne pepper for the marinade
  • Lemon Juice --Having an acidic base helps penetrate the meat

Sauce

  • Coconut oil --Using this Ensures that coconut will be present in every note of the dish
  • Onion --An onion that cooks down well like a white or yellow
  • Aromatics --Fresh ginger and garlic
  • Spices --Ground coriander, curry poweder and turmeric
  • Tomatoes --One large can of diced tomatoes
  • Coconut milk --Rich, delicious and the creamy component of the curry
  • Cilantro --Chopped up nice and fine for garnish. If you don't like cilantro use parsley instead

Instructions

  1. Marinate the shrimp: In a small bowl toss the shrimp with the marinade indgredients. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 10 minutes.
  2. Form the flavor base: While the shrimp is marinating, heat the oil in a medium size skillet. To it add the onion, and cook for 2 or 3 minutes until the onion softens and becomes translucent. Stir in the garlic, ginger, pepper, salt, coriander, turmeric and curry powder. Allow to cook for another minute.
  3. Cook the shrimp: Add the diced tomatoes with juices and all, the coconut milk, stir and bring to a boil. Cook for about 5 minutes stiring occasionally. Add the shrimp with the accumulated juices from the marinade and cook for another 2 minutes or until the shrimp is pink and cooked through.
  4. Finish the curry: Serve over hot rice and garnish with cilantro or parsley.
